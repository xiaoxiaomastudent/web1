How to travel in
Florence?

STEP 1

Consider a time or a season for travel to Florence.

The main high season in Florence is summer and it begins in May, covers June and
July, skips August, and finishes around the end of September. During these time,
there are major events and holidays in Florence for which lots of people travel to

planning your trip to Florence to be able to avoid the highest temperature.

STEP 2

Search your low-cost airlines.

There are many low-cost airlines in Europe. After you decide your travel and you
can immediately look for your ticket. Most discount airlines in Europe sell their
tickets exclusively over their website or over the phone. It is usually cheap when
you fly in the middle of a week, in the early morning or late night.

STEP 3

Strat to do the research and plan your schedule.

Choose the areas you will visit and look at the schedules for art museums and
other attractions. Don't pack your schedule too tight, because maybe there are
too many tourists. There is another way to save your time which is to buy you
ticket online and register a time and you can skip the line. Buy a Amici Degli Card
that includes many museums, gardens and so on. There is some discount for
student, so you need to bring your student ID.

STEP 4

Think about to use public transportation.

Public transportation in Florence is widely used. Buy your tickets before you
board the bus, as the bus driver does not check tickets but you need to put your
ticket in "ticket validating" machine. You can buy tickets from the driver but they
cost more. Usually, people buy tickets at local tobacco shops and small stores
with "T" sign.

Learn a few everyday use words in Italian and
know how to be polite.

before you want to talk in English. Italy is full of churches. Shorts, tank tops, and
